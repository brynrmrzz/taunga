Sustainable Jewelry Making from Ecuadorian Artisans

Tauga is a tree that grows in Ecuador which produces nuts, similar to coconuts, that when cracked open reveals smaller nuts inside that are then harvested and cut with a ban saw into all different designs. After these families complete this part, other families design and dye the ivory pieces with natural dyes, then create the bracelets. It takes 7 families to produce this product. The good part is that the tree is not destroyed and is a sustainable way for them to continue to earn an income.
